Child Safety Standards

MatchCoach • Last updated 7 July 2026

MatchCoach (developed by Pagasis Developers) is committed to protecting children and maintaining a safe platform. In accordance with Google Play's Child Safety Standards policy, we have established the following standards to prevent and prohibit Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation (CSAE), and to comply with all applicable child safety laws.

1. Zero tolerance for CSAE

MatchCoach maintains a strict zero-tolerance policy toward child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) and child sexual abuse material (CSAM). This content is illegal and is absolutely prohibited on our platform. Specifically, we prohibit:

  • The creation, sharing, solicitation, or promotion of child sexual abuse material (CSAM).
  • Any use of the app for grooming, exploitation, trafficking, or any other behavior that endangers children.
  • Any content, communication, or conduct that sexualizes minors or violates child protection laws.

MatchCoach is intended for adult users aged 18 and over. Any account found engaging in CSAE will be permanently banned, and we will report it to the appropriate authorities.

2. In-app reporting mechanism

MatchCoach provides an in-app mechanism for users to submit feedback, concerns, or reports of inappropriate content or behavior, including potential CSAE or CSAM. Reports can also be sent directly to our child safety contact listed below. We review all reports and act promptly.

3. Addressing CSAM

Upon obtaining actual knowledge of CSAM, MatchCoach takes appropriate action in accordance with these standards and applicable law, including the swift removal of the content, banning of the responsible account, and reporting to the relevant authorities such as the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC).

4. Compliance with child safety laws

MatchCoach complies with applicable child safety laws and regulations. We have a process in place to report confirmed CSAM to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and/or relevant local authorities, and we align our practices with global best practices, including those of the Tech Coalition for combating online child sexual exploitation and abuse.

6. Reporting child endangerment

If you believe a child is in danger or has been subjected to abuse, exploitation, or trafficking, please contact your local law enforcement immediately. In the United States, you may also report to the NCMEC CyberTipline at report.cybertip.org.
