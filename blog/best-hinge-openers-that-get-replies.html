Best Hinge Openers That Actually Get Replies (2026)

By MatchCoach • Updated July 2026 • 9 min read

Hinge is designed to be commented on — you can like and reply to a specific photo or prompt. That's a gift, and most people waste it by sending "hey" or liking a photo with no message. If you actually use what's on their profile, you'll out-perform the vast majority of matches instantly.

Here's why generic openers fail, the framework for openers that work, and specific examples for every kind of Hinge prompt and photo.

Why "hi how are you" gets ignored

Picture the other person's inbox: a wall of "hey," "hi gorgeous," and "how's your weekend?" Every one of those looks identical. There's nothing to react to, no hook, no proof you looked at their profile. Replying takes effort and offers no reward, so they don't.

Openers that die in the inbox
"Hey" • "Hi how are you" • "You're gorgeous" • "Happy Friday!" • "What's up"

The framework: Specific + Easy + Light

Every high-reply opener hits three notes:

  • Specific — it references something only on their profile. This proves you read it and makes them feel seen.
  • Easy — it gives them a clear, low-effort way to respond. No homework.
  • Light — it's playful, not intense. First messages should feel fun, not like a job interview.

Remember: the goal of an opener isn't to be impressive. It's to make replying feel easy and fun. Specific beats clever every single time.

Openers by prompt type

"Two truths and a lie"

"Okay I'm 80% sure the lie is the skydiving one — you don't strike me as someone who'd willingly jump out of a plane. Am I right or completely wrong?"

"I go crazy for..."

"You said you go crazy for a good playlist — this feels like a trap but I'm walking into it anyway. What's on heavy rotation right now?"

"My simple pleasures"

"'Coffee before anyone talks to me' might be the most relatable thing I've read on this app. What's the coffee order that defines you?"

"Dating me is like..."

"'Dating me is like a Costco free sample' — I need the full explanation for this immediately, it's either the best or worst pitch I've ever seen"

Openers by photo type

Travel photo

"That photo looks like Portugal but I'm genuinely just guessing to seem worldly. Where actually was it?"

Photo with a pet

"I'm legally required to ask the dog's name before we continue. What are we working with here?"

Activity / hobby photo (hiking, cooking, sports)

"The hiking photos are making me feel guilty about my weekend plans (couch). What's the best trail you've done recently?"

Group photo (and you're not sure which one is them)

"Great photo but you've created a real 'which one are you' mystery here. Going to guess you're the one who organized this whole thing?"

The advanced move: match their energy

Read the tone of their profile before you write. A profile full of dry jokes wants a witty opener. A warm, earnest profile wants something a little more genuine. Sending a savage roast to someone whose whole vibe is sincere lands wrong — and vice versa. Matching their energy is what separates good openers from great ones.
